Project 44: Forecasting With Prophet — Trend Changepoints and Holiday Effects

Prophet Changepoint Detection Fourier Seasonality Holiday Regressors Uncertainty Intervals

Difficulty: Hard  |  Builds on: Projects 25, 26, 36

1 · Hypothetical Problem

  • An e-commerce company needs to forecast daily website traffic for the next 90 days
  • Traffic has a growing trend that changed slope twice (product launches)
  • Strong weekly seasonality (weekdays vs weekends) and annual seasonality (holiday season)
  • Black Friday, Christmas, and summer sales cause massive spikes
  • ARIMA (Project 26) struggles with multiple seasonalities and trend changes
  • Goal → use Prophet to handle changepoints, multiple seasonalities, and holiday effects automatically

2 · How to Approach It

  1. From Project 25: decomposition separates trend, seasonality, residual — Prophet does this as a model
  2. From Project 26: ARIMA handles one seasonality — Prophet handles multiple via Fourier terms
  3. From Project 36: hierarchical forecasting needs coherent forecasts — Prophet can be a base forecaster
  4. Prophet decomposes: y(t) = trend(t) + seasonality(t) + holidays(t) + error(t)
  5. Trend can have automatic changepoints — no need to manually specify when the trend changed

3 · How Can We Solve It

  1. Generate 3 years of daily traffic data with trend changes, weekly/yearly seasonality, and holidays
  2. Fit Prophet with default settings — it auto-detects changepoints and seasonality
  3. Add custom holidays (Black Friday, product launches)
  4. Tune changepoint sensitivity and seasonality strength
  5. Generate forecasts with uncertainty intervals
  6. Compare with ARIMA baseline from Project 26

4 · What Should Do

  • ✅ Inspect the component plots — they show trend, weekly, and yearly patterns separately
  • ✅ Add known holidays and special events — Prophet handles them as additional regressors
  • ✅ Tune changepoint_prior_scale — higher values allow more trend flexibility
  • ✅ Use cross-validation with Prophet's built-in cross_validation function
  • ✅ Check the uncertainty intervals — they should cover ~95% of actual values

5 · What Should NOT Do

  • ❌ Do NOT use Prophet for sub-daily data without careful configuration — it's designed for daily+
  • ❌ Do NOT ignore the changepoint locations — they reveal when the business dynamics shifted
  • ❌ Do NOT set too many changepoints — overfits the trend to noise
  • ❌ Do NOT forget to cap the trend for logistic growth — unbounded growth is unrealistic
  • ❌ Do NOT use Prophet for non-time-series problems — it's specifically for temporal forecasting

6 · When to Use What

  • 🔹 Prophet → daily/weekly data with multiple seasonalities, holidays, and trend changes
  • 🔹 ARIMA (Project 26) → single seasonality, stationary or differenced data, short-term forecasts
  • 🔹 Exponential Smoothing → simple trend + single seasonality; fast and interpretable
  • 🔹 Hierarchical methods (Project 36) → when forecasts must be coherent across levels
  • 🔹 LSTM (Project 49) → when complex nonlinear temporal patterns exist

7 · When NOT to Use

  • 🚫 For very short time series (< 2 full seasonal cycles) — not enough data to estimate seasonality
  • 🚫 For high-frequency data (seconds, minutes) without aggregation
  • 🚫 When the series has no trend or seasonality — simpler methods suffice
  • 🚫 When you need causal explanations — Prophet is a forecasting tool, not a causal model

8 · How It Only Works — Internal Mechanics

Prophet's Decomposition

  • y(t) = g(t) + s(t) + h(t) + ε(t)
  • g(t): trend — piecewise linear or logistic growth with automatic changepoints
  • s(t): seasonality — Fourier series for weekly, yearly, and custom periods
  • h(t): holiday effects — indicator variables with windows around each holiday
  • ε(t): error — assumed normally distributed

Automatic Changepoint Detection

  • Prophet places potential changepoints at evenly spaced locations in the first 80% of data
  • Uses a sparse prior (Laplace) on the rate changes — most changepoints have zero effect
  • changepoint_prior_scale controls flexibility: higher = more changepoints activated
  • The detected changepoints reveal when the trend slope changed

Fourier Seasonality

  • Weekly seasonality: sum of sin/cos terms with period 7 days
  • Yearly seasonality: sum of sin/cos terms with period 365.25 days
  • fourier_order controls complexity: higher = more flexible seasonal patterns
  • This is why Prophet handles multiple seasonalities — each gets its own Fourier series

Uncertainty Intervals

  • Trend uncertainty: simulated from the changepoint posterior
  • Observation uncertainty: from the residual variance
  • Combined: wider intervals further into the future (more uncertain)

9 · Common Pitfalls and Failure Modes

  • ⚠️ Overfitting the trend — too many changepoints make the trend follow noise
  • ⚠️ Missing holidays — unmodeled holidays appear as large residuals; add them explicitly
  • ⚠️ Extrapolating too far — Prophet's trend extrapolation becomes unreliable beyond 1–2 seasonal cycles
  • ⚠️ Ignoring component plots — they're the most valuable diagnostic tool
  • ⚠️ Not using cross-validation — Prophet's built-in CV uses expanding windows appropriate for time series

10 · Scalability Considerations

  • Prophet fits in seconds for typical daily data (1–5 years)
  • For thousands of time series: parallelize with joblib or use Prophet's MAP estimation (faster than MCMC)
  • MCMC mode gives better uncertainty estimates but is 10–100× slower
  • For millions of series: consider simpler models (ETS) or neural approaches (Project 49)

11 · Interpretability Considerations

  • ✅ Component plots are highly interpretable — stakeholders see trend, weekly, yearly patterns separately
  • ✅ Changepoint locations tell a business story — "growth accelerated after the product launch"
  • ✅ Holiday effects are quantified — "Black Friday adds 150% to baseline traffic"
  • ✅ Uncertainty intervals communicate forecast confidence visually

12 · Complete Working Code

import numpy as np
import pandas as pd
from statsmodels.tsa.holtwinters import ExponentialSmoothing
import matplotlib
matplotlib.use('Agg')
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

np.random.seed(42)

# ─── Step 1: Generate daily traffic data with realistic patterns ───
n_days = 1095  # 3 years
dates = pd.date_range('2021-01-01', periods=n_days, freq='D')
t = np.arange(n_days)

# Trend with changepoints
trend = np.piecewise(t, [t < 365, (t >= 365) & (t < 730), t >= 730],
                      [lambda x: 1000 + 2*x,
                       lambda x: 1000 + 2*365 + 5*(x-365),
                       lambda x: 1000 + 2*365 + 5*365 + 1*(x-730)])

# Weekly seasonality (weekends lower)
day_of_week = np.array([d.weekday() for d in dates])
weekly = np.where(day_of_week >= 5, -200, 100)  # weekends drop

# Yearly seasonality
day_of_year = np.array([d.timetuple().tm_yday for d in dates])
yearly = 300 * np.sin(2 * np.pi * day_of_year / 365.25) + 150 * np.cos(4 * np.pi * day_of_year / 365.25)

# Holiday effects
holidays_df = pd.DataFrame({
    'ds': pd.to_datetime(['2021-11-26', '2021-12-25', '2022-11-25',
                           '2022-12-25', '2023-11-24', '2023-12-25']),
    'holiday': ['black_friday', 'christmas'] * 3,
    'effect': [800, 500, 900, 550, 1000, 600]
})

holiday_effect = np.zeros(n_days)
for _, row in holidays_df.iterrows():
    idx = (dates == row['ds'])
    if idx.any():
        pos = np.where(idx)[0][0]
        for offset in range(-2, 3):
            if 0 <= pos + offset < n_days:
                holiday_effect[pos + offset] += row['effect'] * max(0, 1 - abs(offset) * 0.3)

noise = np.random.normal(0, 100, n_days)
traffic = np.maximum(trend + weekly + yearly + holiday_effect + noise, 100)

df = pd.DataFrame({'ds': dates, 'y': traffic})

print("=== Daily Traffic Data ===")
print(f"  Period: {dates[0].date()} to {dates[-1].date()} ({n_days} days)")
print(f"  Mean traffic: {traffic.mean():.0f}, Std: {traffic.std():.0f}")

# ─── Step 2: Train/test split ───
train_end = n_days - 90
train = df.iloc[:train_end].copy()
test = df.iloc[train_end:].copy()
print(f"  Train: {len(train)} days, Test: {len(test)} days")

# ─── Step 3: Prophet-style decomposition (manual implementation) ───
# Since Prophet requires installation, we implement the core concepts manually

# Trend estimation using piecewise linear regression
from sklearn.linear_model import LinearRegression

# Detect changepoints using residual analysis
n_changepoints = 10
cp_indices = np.linspace(0, len(train) * 0.8, n_changepoints + 2, dtype=int)[1:-1]

# Build changepoint design matrix
A = np.zeros((len(train), n_changepoints))
for j, cp in enumerate(cp_indices):
    A[cp:, j] = np.arange(len(train) - cp) if cp < len(train) else 0
    A[cp:, j] = np.arange(1, len(train) - cp + 1)

X_trend = np.column_stack([np.arange(len(train)), A])
trend_model = LinearRegression()
trend_model.fit(X_trend, train['y'].values)
trend_fitted = trend_model.predict(X_trend)

# Identify significant changepoints
cp_magnitudes = np.abs(trend_model.coef_[1:])
significant_cps = cp_indices[cp_magnitudes > np.percentile(cp_magnitudes, 70)]

print(f"\n=== Changepoint Detection ===")
print(f"  Candidate changepoints: {n_changepoints}")
print(f"  Significant changepoints: {len(significant_cps)}")
for cp in significant_cps:
    print(f"    {train['ds'].iloc[cp].date()}")

# ─── Step 4: Seasonality estimation using Fourier terms ───
# Weekly seasonality (Fourier order 3)
weekly_fourier = np.column_stack([
    np.sin(2 * np.pi * k * np.arange(len(train)) / 7) for k in range(1, 4)
] + [
    np.cos(2 * np.pi * k * np.arange(len(train)) / 7) for k in range(1, 4)
])

# Yearly seasonality (Fourier order 5)
yearly_fourier = np.column_stack([
    np.sin(2 * np.pi * k * np.arange(len(train)) / 365.25) for k in range(1, 6)
] + [
    np.cos(2 * np.pi * k * np.arange(len(train)) / 365.25) for k in range(1, 6)
])

# Combined model
X_full = np.column_stack([X_trend, weekly_fourier, yearly_fourier])
full_model = LinearRegression()
full_model.fit(X_full, train['y'].values)
train_pred = full_model.predict(X_full)

# ─── Step 5: Forecast ───
t_test = np.arange(len(train), len(train) + len(test))
A_test = np.zeros((len(test), n_changepoints))
for j, cp in enumerate(cp_indices):
    if cp < len(train):
        A_test[:, j] = np.arange(len(train) - cp + 1, len(train) - cp + len(test) + 1)

X_trend_test = np.column_stack([t_test, A_test])

weekly_test = np.column_stack([
    np.sin(2 * np.pi * k * t_test / 7) for k in range(1, 4)
] + [
    np.cos(2 * np.pi * k * t_test / 7) for k in range(1, 4)
])

yearly_test = np.column_stack([
    np.sin(2 * np.pi * k * t_test / 365.25) for k in range(1, 6)
] + [
    np.cos(2 * np.pi * k * t_test / 365.25) for k in range(1, 6)
])

X_test_full = np.column_stack([X_trend_test, weekly_test, yearly_test])
forecast = full_model.predict(X_test_full)

# Uncertainty intervals
residuals = train['y'].values - train_pred
residual_std = np.std(residuals)
forecast_lower = forecast - 1.96 * residual_std
forecast_upper = forecast + 1.96 * residual_std

# Evaluate
from sklearn.metrics import mean_absolute_error, mean_squared_error
mae = mean_absolute_error(test['y'].values, forecast)
rmse = np.sqrt(mean_squared_error(test['y'].values, forecast))
coverage = np.mean((test['y'].values >= forecast_lower) & (test['y'].values <= forecast_upper))

print(f"\n=== Forecast Results (90-day horizon) ===")
print(f"  MAE: {mae:.0f}")
print(f"  RMSE: {rmse:.0f}")
print(f"  95% CI coverage: {coverage:.1%}")

# ─── Step 6: Visualization ───
fig, axes = plt.subplots(2, 2, figsize=(14, 12))

# Full series with forecast
ax = axes[0, 0]
ax.plot(train['ds'], train['y'], color='#c9d1d9', linewidth=0.5, alpha=0.7, label='Train')
ax.plot(test['ds'], test['y'], color='white', linewidth=1, label='Actual')
ax.plot(test['ds'], forecast, color='#58a6ff', linewidth=2, label='Forecast')
ax.fill_between(test['ds'], forecast_lower, forecast_upper, alpha=0.2, color='#58a6ff')
ax.set_title('Traffic Forecast with Uncertainty')
ax.legend(fontsize=8)
ax.grid(True, alpha=0.2)

# Trend component
ax = axes[0, 1]
ax.plot(train['ds'], trend_fitted, color='#f0883e', linewidth=2)
for cp in significant_cps:
    ax.axvline(train['ds'].iloc[cp], color='#f85149', linestyle='--', alpha=0.5)
ax.set_title('Trend Component + Changepoints')
ax.grid(True, alpha=0.2)

# Weekly seasonality
weekly_effect = weekly_fourier @ full_model.coef_[n_changepoints+1:n_changepoints+7]
avg_weekly = pd.Series(weekly_effect, index=train['ds']).groupby(train['ds'].dt.dayofweek).mean()
days = ['Mon', 'Tue', 'Wed', 'Thu', 'Fri', 'Sat', 'Sun']
axes[1, 0].bar(days, avg_weekly.values, color='#3fb950', alpha=0.8)
axes[1, 0].set_ylabel('Effect on Traffic')
axes[1, 0].set_title('Weekly Seasonality')
axes[1, 0].grid(True, alpha=0.2, axis='y')

# Residual distribution
axes[1, 1].hist(residuals, bins=50, color='#bc8cff', alpha=0.7, density=True, edgecolor='white')
axes[1, 1].set_xlabel('Residual')
axes[1, 1].set_ylabel('Density')
axes[1, 1].set_title(f'Residuals (std={residual_std:.0f})')
axes[1, 1].grid(True, alpha=0.2)

plt.tight_layout()
plt.savefig('project_44_prophet_forecast.png', dpi=100, bbox_inches='tight')
print(f"\n✅ Prophet-style forecast saved as project_44_prophet_forecast.png")
